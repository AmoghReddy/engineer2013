Description

TechSpeak is a series of a video conferences with eminent personalities who are working at the frontier of what possible with technology. In the past we have managed to interact with individuals like John C. Mather, who won the Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_Background_Explorer, Bjarne Stroustrup who was the creator C,C++, Sam Pitroda who has been the advisor to the Prime Minister of India on public information infrastructure.
This year the aim of TechSpeak is to showcase speakers who can give viewers a precious insight into what the world needs from engineers and what engineers can do for the world.

Schedule

Banibrata Mukopadhyay

- Associate Professor of Astrophysics at IISC, Bangalore. He calculated the mass limit to which white dwarf stars can reach, before they collapse into neutron stars or black holes. Refuting previously believed limit that was proposed by Chandrasekhar. (Oct. 26th 4.30pm - 6.00pm)
2. Max Little

- Wellcome Trust/MIT Fellow and TED Fellow. Mathematician with a background in applied mathematics, statistics, signal processing and computational engineering. Research work has been applied to problems in biomedicine (such as monitoring of Parkinson's disease and voice pathologies), extreme rainfall analysis and forecasting, biophysical signal processing, and hydrogeomorphology and open channel flow measurement. (Oct. 25th 7.30pm - 8.30pm)
3. K. VijayRaghavan

- Director of The National Centre for Biological Sciences. He is the recipient of the Infosys Prize in the life sciences (2009) and was conferred the honour of the Padma Shri in 2013. His fields of specialization are Developmental Biology, Genetics and Neurogenetics. His research primarily focuses on the important principles and mechanisms that control nervous systems and muscles during development. (Oct. 27th 3.30pm - 4.30pm)
